The History of La Union

⚓ A Province Born from Union

Founded in 1850, La Union's name comes from its creation through the union of three provinces

🏛️ Spanish Era

La Union was officially established on March 2, 1850, by the Spanish colonial government to improve civil administration and defense. Its name literally translates to "the Union, " symbolizing the merger of the southern towns of Ilocos Sur, the northern towns of Pangasinan, and the western towns of Benguet.

🌊 Surf Culture

San Juan became the surfing capital in the 1980s, hosting international competitions yearly.

🎨 Heritage & Arts

Home to the famous "Basi" wine, inabel weaving, and the vibrant "Pindang" Festival.

Cultural Hub

🎨Language

Language: True to its rich historical melting pot of Ilocano, Pangasinense, and Cordilleran cultures, La Union was notably the first province in the Philippines to declare Ilocano a co-official language.
